Min Liu

I am a 4th year undergraduate in Kuang Yaming Honors School, Nanjing University. My research interest lies in the intersection of artificial intelligence and robotics. My long-term goal is to build general-purpose robotic systems that perform a diverse range of tasks in a large variety of environments in the physical world.

Previously, I worked on quality assurance for machine learning, advised by Dr. Xiangyu Yue (currently assistant professor at CUHK), Prof. Alberto Sangiovanni Vincentelli at UC Berkeley iCyPhy and Prof. Yuan Yao at NJU ICS. I also worked on a few topics in NLP, including parallel text generation, advised by Prof. Shujian Huang and Dr. Yu Bao at NJUNLP, and cross-modal pre-training for robust speech and text representations, advised by Dr. Chengqi Zhao at ByteDance AI Lab.

In the spring of 2023, I will start my first research on robot learning, supervised by Prof. Lin Shao at National University of Singapore.
