Defense Against Neural Trojan Attacks

  • Institute of Computer Software, Nanjing University
    2020.9 - 2021.5
  • Supervisor: Professor Yuan Yao
  • Description
    In trojan attacks, the adversary will insert malicious functionality which will be activated in the presence of specific inputs into the neural network. We were developing a method to clean the dataset which potentially contains malicious inputs. This method frees us from dealing with the attacked models. Performance of the method is not satisfying enough by far. Since it is not my idea, I am not supposed to tell more details about the method here.
  • Main Contributions
    I was mainly responsible for experimenting with some of the models developed by a graduate student Tong Wang using PyTorch and Tensorflow. I also devised some techniques to improve the performace of the model. On top of that, I was responsible for a few reports relevant to trojan attacks and intelligent coding.
  • Review
    As my first research experience, this project leaves me many happy memories. One of my teachers in the Problem Solving course, Hengfeng Wei, recommended Professor Yuan Yao to me. They are both very kind and generous. Professor Yao spent hours telling me basic knowledge about AI research in our first meeting. He also introduced the graduate student Tong Wang to me, who taught me a lot about implementation of deep learning in the second semester. I spent a lot of time learning classical machine learning algorithms and deep learning by myself in the first few months, and it was exciting to apply the algorithms to solve problems in the project.
    It was tough as a beginner, but finally I am equipped with knowledge and confidence to explore more about these fields. My experience in ICS get me prepared for the second journey in Econometrics, which is really challenging and intensive. In general, I believe that this is a wonderful start point.
